The country became a crucial center for trade, culture, and learning during ancient and medieval times. With the arrival of European powers and the eventual colonization by the British, India faced nearly 200 years of foreign rule. The struggle for independence, led by figures like Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, Subhas Chandra Bose, and countless other freedom fighters, culminated in India gaining independence on August 15, 1947. Post-independence, India adopted a democratic system and became a sovereign republic on January 26, 1950. India is the most populous country in the world as of 2024, with an estimated population of around 1.44 billion people. This number has been steadily increasing over the decades due to high birth rates and improvements in healthcare. In 2011, according to the Census of India, the population was approximately 1.21 billion. By 2015, it had grown to 1.31 billion, and by 2020, it reached around 1.38 billion. In 2022, India had an estimated population of 1.41 billion, and in 2023, it slightly increased to 1.43 billion. Future projections suggest that by 2030, the population may rise to 1.50 billion, and by 2040, it could reach 1.58 billion. These figures highlight the importance of planning for resources, infrastructure, and employment to support the growing population.

India has made significant progress in reducing poverty over the past two decades. In 2005, over 37% of the population was living below the poverty line. However, due to economic growth, welfare programs, and rural development schemes, the poverty rate started declining. By 2011, the poverty rate had dropped to 21.9%, according to government data. In 2015, it was estimated at around 13.4%. Despite setbacks during the COVID-19 pandemic, which temporarily increased economic hardship, the situation began to improve again post-2021. By 2022, the poverty rate was estimated at around 9.1%, and in 2023, it was reduced further to approximately 8.5%. In 2024, the poverty rate is estimated to be close to 8%, reflecting India's continued efforts to improve the living conditions of its citizens.
